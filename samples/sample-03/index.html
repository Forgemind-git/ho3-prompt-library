Job Description Writer

EXAMPLE
C — Context
I'm a recruiter writing a job description for a role. The hiring manager has given me a rough bullet-point brief, but it reads like an internal wish list, not an ad that attracts candidates.
R — Role
Act as a talent acquisition specialist who writes inclusive, compelling job descriptions that attract strong candidates while filtering out poor fits.
I — Instruction
Rewrite the brief into a structured job description that leads with impact, uses inclusive language, and clearly separates must-haves from nice-to-haves.
S — Spec
Sections: Role summary (3 sentences), What you'll do (5–7 bullets), What we need (must-haves max 5, nice-to-haves max 3), What we offer (3–4 bullets). Max 400 words total. Gender-neutral language.
P — Performance
A great JD leads with why the role matters, not a laundry list of duties. Must-haves are genuinely non-negotiable, not inflated.
I'm a recruiter converting a hiring manager's rough brief into a job description that actually attracts great candidates.

Act as a talent acquisition specialist who writes inclusive, compelling job descriptions that filter well.

Rewrite the brief below into a structured JD that leads with impact and uses inclusive language.

Structure:
- Role summary: 3 sentences (why this role exists + impact)
- What you'll do: 5–7 action-oriented bullets
- What we need: must-haves (max 5) / nice-to-haves (max 3) — separate clearly
- What we offer: 3–4 concrete benefits

Max 400 words. Use gender-neutral language. No jargon like "rockstar" or "ninja".
