# Prompt 08 — Content Calendar Brief

**Library:** Content Marketer Prompt Library  
**Role:** Content Marketing Manager

---

## Purpose

Brief a month of content for a specific campaign or theme — providing a structured calendar with content types, angles, distribution channels, and an editorial through-line that makes the month feel cohesive rather than a random collection of posts.

---

## CRISP Breakdown

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Context** | Month: [month and year]. Campaign/theme: [the focus for this month]. Company: [name, product]. Key launch or event: [any product launch, webinar, campaign happening this month]. Channels active: [list — blog, LinkedIn, email, etc.]. |
| **Role** | You are a content director who plans monthly calendars that have an editorial theme running through them — so each piece reinforces the others, rather than being standalone isolated posts. |
| **Instruction** | Create a month-long content brief with: editorial theme statement, one anchor piece, weekly content plan per channel, and a note on how each piece ladders to the anchor. |
| **Spec** | Calendar format with weekly table (Week 1–4). Each week: 1 blog topic / 2 social posts / 1 email send (or adjust to channels specified). Each piece has a one-line angle, not just a topic. |
| **Performance** | Content team can start briefing and producing immediately. Each piece has a clear editorial angle. The month tells a coherent story when read in sequence. |

---

## Full Prompt

```
Context: I'm planning content for [Company Name] for [Month + Year]. 
- Campaign/theme for this month: [the main editorial focus — e.g., "retention month", "new product launch", "thought leadership push"]
- Key event or launch: [any anchor event — product launch date, webinar, conference, or "none"]
- Target audience this month: [who you're primarily targeting with this content]
- Active channels: [list — blog / LinkedIn / email newsletter / Twitter / Instagram / YouTube]
- Content team capacity: [how many pieces can be produced — e.g., "4 blog posts, 12 social posts, 4 email sends"]

Role: Act as a content director who builds monthly calendars with a clear editorial through-line — so each piece reinforces the others and the month tells a coherent story, not a random selection of topics.

Instruction: Create a monthly content brief with:
1. Editorial Theme Statement — one sentence that describes the month's narrative arc
2. Anchor Piece — the most important piece of content this month (usually a long-form blog or video) that everything else can reference
3. Weekly Plan (Weeks 1–4) — for each week:
   - One blog/long-form topic with angle
   - 2–3 social post topics with platform and angle
   - 1 email topic with subject line direction
4. How It Ladders — brief note on how each week builds on the previous one
5. Repurpose Map — which pieces can be turned into what (e.g., blog post → 3 social posts → email section)

Spec:
- Calendar table format for the weekly plan
- Each piece has an angle (one sentence), not just a topic title
- Anchor piece defined upfront
- Repurpose map at the end
- Adapt to the channel list and capacity provided

Performance bar: Content team can start briefing the first week's pieces immediately. Each piece's angle is specific enough to be a brief. Month has a coherent narrative when consumed in sequence.
```

---

## Before Example (Weak Prompt)

**Prompt used:**
> Plan a month of marketing content about customer support.

**Output produced:**
> Week 1: Blog post about customer support tips. Week 2: Social media posts about customer service. Week 3: Email newsletter. Week 4: Another blog post.

**Why it's weak:** No angles, no editorial through-line, no channel specificity, "tips" and "about customer service" are not briefs. A writer receiving this has no idea what to produce.

---

## After Example (With CRISP)

**Placeholders filled:**
- Company: Helply | Month: July 2025
- Theme: The CSAT myth — why retention leaders are changing what they measure
- Key event: Webinar on retention metrics, July 17
- Audience: CS leaders at B2B SaaS
- Channels: Blog (2 posts), LinkedIn (8 posts), Email (4 sends)

**Output produced:**

> **July 2025 Content Brief — Helply**  
> Editorial Theme: "The retention metric rethink" — July is about challenging the industry's over-reliance on CSAT and introducing a better measurement philosophy, culminating in the July 17 webinar.
>
> **Anchor Piece**  
> Blog: "Why Your CSAT Score Is Lying to You (And What to Measure Instead)"  
> — 2,000-word definitive guide to the CSAT-churn paradox, with Helply's data, alternative metrics, and a framework for the transition. Publishes July 7. Everything in July references this.
>
> ---
>
> **Weekly Plan**
>
> | | Blog | LinkedIn (2x) | Email |
> |-|------|---------------|-------|
> | **Week 1 (Jul 1–6)** | Teaser: "The Problem With How We Measure Customer Happiness" — 800-word opinion piece setting up the problem without solving it yet | Post 1: "63% of our churned customers gave 5-star CSAT" (data post) | Pre-webinar announcement: "Something we've been meaning to say about CSAT" |
> | **Week 2 (Jul 7–13)** | **ANCHOR: "Why Your CSAT Is Lying to You"** | Post 1: CSAT vs. intent metrics explainer (key takeaway from anchor) | Deliver anchor piece to subscribers |
> | | | Post 2: Quote card from anchor ("The better question is not...") | |
> | **Week 3 (Jul 14–20)** | None — webinar week | Post 1: Webinar preview — "3 metrics we're going to challenge on Thursday" | Webinar reminder (2 days before) + post-webinar recap with recording link |
> | | | Post 2: Live thread or recap from webinar | |
> | **Week 4 (Jul 21–31)** | "How to Start Tracking Intent Metrics: A Practical Guide" — actionable follow-on to anchor | Post 1: Testimonial from webinar participant | "What we learned from the webinar — and the one thing that surprised us most" |
> | | | Post 2: "The 3 questions to ask before your next renewal call" | |
>
> **How It Ladders**
> - Week 1: Establish the problem (creates tension, builds curiosity)
> - Week 2: Deliver the anchor and the argument (peak content week)
> - Week 3: Convert interest into webinar attendance (action week)
> - Week 4: Extend the conversation and drive product CTA
>
> **Repurpose Map**
> - Anchor blog → 4 LinkedIn posts (one per major section) → 2 email feature sections
> - Webinar → Recording clip for LinkedIn → Blog recap → Email send
