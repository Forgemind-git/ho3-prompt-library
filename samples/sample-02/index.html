Blog Post Ideation

EXAMPLE
C — Context
I'm a solo content marketer for a B2B SaaS company. I publish two blog posts per week and struggle to come up with fresh angles that haven't been done to death.
R — Role
Act as a content strategist who specialises in B2B SaaS thought leadership and SEO-driven editorial planning.
I — Instruction
Generate 10 blog post ideas on the topic I give you. Each idea should have a unique angle that hasn't been covered to death.
S — Spec
Return a numbered list. Each item: title + one-line rationale (why this angle is fresh). Keep each item under 30 words. No generic "ultimate guide" titles.
P — Performance
A great output gives me titles I haven't seen before, with angles grounded in real reader pain — not SEO-keyword stuffing.
I'm a solo content marketer for a B2B SaaS company publishing two posts/week. I need fresh angles, not recycled takes.

Act as a content strategist specialising in B2B SaaS thought leadership and SEO-driven editorial planning.

Generate 10 blog post ideas on the topic below. Each must have a distinct angle not commonly covered.

Return a numbered list. Format per item:
- Title (compelling, specific)
- One-line rationale: why this angle is fresh and valuable (max 25 words)

No "ultimate guides" or generic listicles. Avoid titles that start with "How to".

Topic: [PASTE YOUR TOPIC]

SEO Title + Meta

READY
C — Context
I've finished a blog post and need a search-optimised title tag and meta description that win the click on Google without misleading readers.
R — Role
Act as an SEO copywriter who balances keyword targeting with human click-through psychology for B2B SaaS content.
I — Instruction
Write 5 title-tag options and 3 meta-description options for the post and target keyword I provide below.
S — Spec
Titles under 60 characters, meta descriptions 140–155 characters; include the target keyword naturally near the front; no clickbait or all-caps.
P — Performance
A great output reads like a person wrote it, earns the click with a clear benefit, and stays within character limits so nothing truncates in the SERP.
I've written a blog post and need SEO metadata that ranks and earns clicks without over-promising.

Act as an SEO copywriter who balances keyword targeting with human click-through psychology for B2B SaaS.

Write:
- 5 title-tag options (under 60 characters each)
- 3 meta-description options (140–155 characters each)

Rules:
- Include the target keyword naturally, ideally near the front
- Lead with a concrete benefit, not hype
- No clickbait, no ALL-CAPS, no truncation past the limits

Target keyword: [TARGET KEYWORD]
Post topic / summary: [PASTE BLOG POST OR SUMMARY]

LinkedIn Carousel Script

READY
C — Context
I want to turn one core idea into a swipeable LinkedIn carousel that stops the scroll and builds my authority in my niche.
R — Role
Act as a LinkedIn content creator who writes punchy, slide-by-slide carousels that earn saves and comments.
I — Instruction
Turn the topic below into an 8–10 slide carousel script, writing the exact text for each slide.
S — Spec
Label each slide (Slide 1, Slide 2…), keep slide text under 20 words, open with a scroll-stopping hook and end with a clear CTA; conversational tone, no jargon.
P — Performance
A great output has a hook I'd genuinely stop for, one idea per slide, and a final slide that prompts saves, comments, or a follow.
I want to turn one idea into a swipeable LinkedIn carousel that stops the scroll and builds authority in my niche.

Act as a LinkedIn content creator who writes punchy, slide-by-slide carousels that earn saves and comments.

Write an 8–10 slide carousel script. Format:
- Slide 1: scroll-stopping hook
- Slides 2–9: one idea each, max 20 words of slide text
- Final slide: a clear call to action (comment, save, or follow)

Tone: conversational, confident, no corporate jargon. Add a short caption suggestion under the slides.

Video Script Outline

READY
C — Context
I'm scripting a short-form video (YouTube Short / Reel / TikTok) and need a structure that hooks in the first 3 seconds and retains viewers.
R — Role
Act as a short-form video scriptwriter who understands retention curves, pattern interrupts, and spoken-word pacing.
I — Instruction
Build a section-by-section script outline for a 60–90 second video on the topic I provide below.
S — Spec
Break it into Hook, Setup, Value (2–3 beats), and CTA; give the spoken line plus a quick visual/B-roll note for each; conversational, say-it-out-loud tone.
P — Performance
A great output has a hook worth 3 seconds, beats that flow when read aloud, and visual cues that make filming obvious.
I'm scripting a 60–90 second short-form video and need a structure that hooks in 3 seconds and keeps viewers watching.

Act as a short-form video scriptwriter who understands retention, pattern interrupts, and spoken-word pacing.

Build a section-by-section script outline with these sections:
- Hook (first 3 seconds)
- Setup (why this matters)
- Value (2–3 punchy beats)
- CTA

For each section give:
- The exact spoken line(s)
- A short visual / B-roll note in brackets

Keep it conversational and easy to say out loud. No teleprompter-stiff phrasing.

Topic: [TOPIC]
Platform: [YOUTUBE SHORT / REEL / TIKTOK]

Act as a content analyst who turns messy performance numbers into clear, action-focused takeaways for a solo marketer.
I — Instruction
Review the metrics I paste below and tell me what worked, what didn't, and what to do differently next month.
S — Spec
Return three sections — Wins, Misses, Next Month's 3 Priorities — using plain language and only conclusions the data supports; flag where data is missing.
P — Performance
A great output gives me 3 concrete changes I can act on this week, each tied to a number, with no vague advice or invented trends.
